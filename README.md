### 🔭 Currently building

**Audience-First Creative Reasoning** — an open research project exploring how creative decisions can be translated into structured, explainable reasoning.

At the heart of the project is a methodology built around a sequence of **Reasoning Gates**. Each gate answers one critical question before moving to the next. Some gates act as prerequisites and therefore carry greater weight because they determine whether subsequent evaluation is meaningful.

The long-term ambition is to evolve this work into an AI-assisted reasoning engine for evaluating static creative artwork.

### 🎯 Who this is for

The methodology is intended for professionals responsible for evaluating and improving creative work, including:

* Creative Directors

The goal is to provide a more structured way to reason about creative effectiveness and support better decision-making.

## Why this matters

Creative reviews often rely on intuition.

While intuition is valuable, it can also make creative decisions difficult to teach, scale, or evaluate consistently across teams.

This project explores whether creative judgment can be broken down into observable signals that connect visual design, audience psychology, and measurable business outcomes.

## Current focus

Version 1 focuses on **static creative artwork**, including:

* Streaming key art
